SAFETY & SUPPORT PLAYBOOK

Prepared for Our Mutual Aid & Chosen Family Circle
For readiness, protection, and collective care in times of rising authoritarianism.

  • Legal Preparedness
    • Carry “Know Your Rights” cards (ACLU, NILC, Transgender Law Center)
    • Consult a trusted immigration lawyer and prepare documentation
    • Assign Power of Attorney if needed
    • Save civil rights & immigration defense contacts in your phone and at home
  • Emergency Documentation Folder
    • Store securely (physically and digitally):
      • IDs: Passport, driver’s license, green card, birth certificates
      • Legal: POA, custody documents, marriage/domestic partnership
      • Health: Insurance cards, prescriptions, allergies
      • Contacts: Family, lawyer, mutual aid circle
  • Safe Communication Plan
    • Use encrypted apps for sensitive info: Signal, ProtonMail
    • Create codewords for danger, regrouping, or pickups
    • Share affirming name/pronouns for queer/trans members
  • Build Your Circle
    • Create a trusted mutual aid pod (legal, medical, emotional, caregiving)
  • Resource Pooling
    • Shared emergency fund
    • Housing/safe stays, ride networks, emergency food & supplies
  • Care Agreements
    • Backup plans for:
      • Childcare, elder care, pet care
      • Medication access (HRT, PrEP, insulin)
      • Emergency transport or housing
  • Protest Preparedness
    • Write legal aid # on your body
    • Disable Face/Touch ID and bring minimal ID if safe
    • Pack water, snacks, phone charger, med supplies
  • Surveillance Awareness
    • Avoid facial recognition zones, live streaming
    • Turn off location services; cover identifying features
    • Use aliases or stage names in high-risk spaces
  • Public Platform Safety
    • Separate public/private accounts if possible
    • Use pseudonyms or stage names to protect legal identity
    • Be mindful about what is shared live vs. posted later
    • Archive public posts and set boundaries to avoid burnout
  • Device Protection
    • Use strong passwords + 2FA
    • Disable location tracking and auto-backups on sensitive apps
    • Encrypt phones and laptops when possible
  • Social Media Hygiene
    • Lock down privacy settings
    • Archive or remove vulnerable content
    • Don’t tag others without consent
    • Monitor for impersonation, back up public posts
  • Mental Health First Aid
    • Trauma-informed, queer-affirming therapist if accessible
    • Care rituals: grounding, breathing, movement
    • Peer check-ins and support pods
  • Spiritual & Cultural Anchoring
    • Maintain rituals, altars, ancestor work, music, dance, prayer
    • Stay rooted in your lineages of resistance and resilience
  • Movement as Readiness
    • Build basic endurance, strength, and flexibility
    • Focus on real-world mobility: running, lifting, climbing, grip
    • Learn to train with minimal equipment (bodyweight, resistance bands)
  • Movement as Mental Health
    • Practice movement for emotional regulation: dance, martial arts, skating, yoga, lifting etc.
    • Use physical activity to discharge stress and reconnect with your body
    • Ritualize your workouts as part of your spiritual/mental care
  • Train with Others
    • Build fitness pods with friends
    • Share routines, check in, and train collectively when possible
    • Center inclusion and accessibility
  • Safety in Strength
    • Strong, mobile, calm bodies are harder to suppress
    • Train not for perfection, but for presence, protection, and collective endurance
  • Exit Strategy
    • Identify safe relocation options
    • Secure travel documents, ID, and emergency funds
    • Research LGBTQIA+-safe regions or sanctuary cities
  • Documentation & Accountability
    • Log police/military encounters, abuse, or rights violations
    • Securely store incident reports, videos, ID photos
    • Back up files offline or using encrypted cloud tools
✨ We care for each other not just in crisis—but always. This is how we survive, resist, and build the future we deserve. You are not alone.
